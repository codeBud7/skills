# Feedback note — SBI structure and Radical Candor

Use with [`SKILL.md`](SKILL.md) for inputs, retrieval, and gates.

## Radical Candor

Reuse the stance from [`1on1/reference.md`](../1on1/reference.md).

## Feedback types

**Praise:** Lead with specific behavior and impact. Reinforce what to keep doing.

**Constructive:** Name the situation and observable behavior; state impact on team, work, or trust; end with a clear ask or next step. Pair care with directness.

**Upward:** Frame for a manager or skip-level. Focus on systemic impact, not personal attacks. State what you need or what would help.

## Output headings

Use short paragraphs under these headings:

`Context`

`Situation`

`Behavior`

`Impact`

`What I'm asking for`

`Missing context` (only when gaps matter)

`Sources` (only when Home OS or linked sources were used)

Under **`Situation`**, anchor to a specific time and place when the user provides one. Do not invent dates or meetings.

Under **`Behavior`**, describe observable actions — what they said or did — not character labels.

Under **`Impact`**, state effect on work, team, customers, or trust. For praise, name the positive outcome. For constructive, name the cost or risk.

Under **`What I'm asking for`**, end with one clear ask, commitment, or appreciation. For praise, this can be "keep doing X" or recognition to share.

Use lightweight source tags like `[profile]`, `[meeting]`, `[user-stated]` when sources were used.

## Style

Short paragraphs. No tables. Facts over interpretation.

Match the direct's feedback preference from their profile when known (e.g. direct vs written, public praise vs private challenge).
